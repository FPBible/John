Ephesians 6

Standing Firm · Armor as Identity · Prayer Without Fear

Paraphrase — Household Faithfulness (Eph 6:1–9)

6:1–3 Children, obey your parents in the Lord, for this is right. “Honor your father and mother” — which is the first commandment with a promise — “so that it may go well with you and you may live long in the land.”

6:4 Fathers, do not provoke your children to anger, but bring them up in the instruction and guidance of the Lord.

6:5–8 Slaves, obey your earthly masters with respect and sincerity of heart, as you would Christ — not with eye-service as people-pleasers, but as servants of Christ, doing the will of God from the heart. Serve with goodwill, as to the Lord and not to people, knowing that whatever good anyone does, this he will receive back from the Lord.

6:9 And masters, do the same to them. Stop threatening, knowing that both their Master and yours is in heaven, and there is no partiality with Him.

Paraphrase — Standing in What Is Already True (Eph 6:10–17)

6:10–12 Finally, be strengthened in the Lord and in the power of His might. Put on the whole armor of God, so that you may be able to stand against the schemes of the adversary. For our struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against rulers, authorities, the powers of this present darkness, and spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly places.

6:13 Therefore take up the whole armor of God, so that you may be able to withstand in the evil day, and having done everything, to stand.

6:14–17 Stand therefore: having fastened the belt of truth, put on the breastplate of righteousness, having shod your feet with the readiness of the gospel of peace; taking up the shield of faith, with which you can extinguish all the flaming arrows of the evil one; and take the helmet of salvation, and the sword of the Spirit — which is the word of God.

Paraphrase — Prayer From Confidence, Not Panic (Eph 6:18–24)

6:18–20 With every prayer and request, pray at all times in the Spirit, staying alert with all perseverance and requests for all the saints. Pray also for me, that words may be given to me when I open my mouth, to boldly make known the mystery of the gospel, for which I am an ambassador in chains — that I may declare it boldly, as I should.

6:21–24 Tychicus, the beloved brother and faithful servant in the Lord, will make everything known to you. Peace be to the brothers and sisters, and love with faith, from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ. Grace be with all who love our Lord Jesus Christ with an incorruptible love.

Immediate Context (Who / What / Why)

Ephesians 6 closes the letter by reinforcing stability. Paul does not introduce new threats. He tells believers how to remain grounded as the old world’s pressures continue to collapse.

“Standing” is the posture of those already seated with Christ. This is not preparation for a future battle, but steadiness in a present reality.

Fulfilled Commentary (New Creation Lens)

1) Household instructions continue the reordering of power

Paul does not endorse abuse or domination. He relativises every authority under Christ and removes absolute power by insisting on mutual accountability.

2) The struggle is not people

Paul explicitly says the conflict is not “flesh and blood.” Blame-shifting onto people contradicts the gospel. The issue is oppressive patterns and accusatory systems.

3) “Adversary” language is functional, not mythological

The adversary works through schemes — lies, accusation, fear, division. Paul is not teaching demon-chasing; he is teaching discernment.

4) The armor is borrowed identity

Each piece describes what believers already have in Christ: truth, righteousness, peace, faith, salvation, and the word. Armor is not summoned — it is worn.

5) “Evil day” refers to covenant pressure

This is not predicting a future apocalypse. It describes moments of testing as the old order resists being displaced.

6) Standing replaces striving

Paul repeats “stand.” There is no call to advance, attack, or fear. Victory is assumed. Stability is the goal.

7) Prayer flows from mission, not anxiety

Paul asks for clarity and boldness, not protection or escape. Prayer partners with God’s purpose rather than reacting to imagined threats.

Key Idioms & Language Notes

  • “Stand” — remain steady in a finished reality.
  • “Armor of God” — identity and vocation sourced from Christ.
  • “Adversary” — accusatory influence, not equal rival.
  • “Heavenly places” — authority realm, not geography.
  • “Sword of the Spirit” — truth spoken against lies.

Prophecy Fulfilment

Ephesians 6 assumes fulfillment. The Messiah reigns, the powers are subordinated, and the people of God are called to remain steady in a world losing its old structures.

New Creation Reality (Today)

You do not fight for victory. You stand in it.

Spiritual maturity is not intensity. It is stability — living truthfully, peacefully, and confidently as someone already seated with Christ.

Reflection

Where am I still reacting with fear or striving, instead of standing in what Christ has already secured?
