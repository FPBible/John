Ephesians 6

Standing Firm in a Finished Victory

Original text: Ephesians 6 — WEB

Immediate Context

Paul concludes by showing how New Creation life expresses itself in ordinary relationships and how believers remain steady as the old covenant world collapses around them.

This chapter does not introduce a new battle. It teaches believers how to stand in a victory already secured in Christ.

Fulfilled Paraphrase

New Creation Order in Households (6:1–9)

Children, listen to your parents in the Lord, for this reflects what is right.

“Honour your father and mother” — this was the first commandment with a promise —

“so that it may go well with you and you may live long in the land.”

Fulfilled clarification: Paul applies the command relationally, not covenantally. The Law is not reinstated; its wisdom is carried forward into New Creation life.

Fathers, do not provoke your children to anger, but raise them with care, instruction, and encouragement in the Lord.

Bondservants, obey your earthly masters with respect and sincerity, as you would Christ —

not merely to please people when watched, but as servants of Christ, doing God’s will from the heart.

Masters, treat them the same way, without threats, knowing that both of you share one Master in heaven, and He shows no partiality.

Fulfilled clarification: Paul addresses existing social structures without endorsing them. The gospel plants the seeds that ultimately dismantle domination and exploitation.

Stand Firm — The Conflict Is Not Flesh (6:10–13)

Finally, be strengthened in the Lord and in the strength of His might.

Put on the full armour of God, so that you are able to stand against the schemes of the adversary.

For our struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against the rulers, authorities, powers, and spiritual forces associated with the old age.

Therefore, take up the full armour of God, so that you may be able to stand in the day of pressure, and having done everything, to remain standing.

Fulfilled clarification: This is not a call to fight unseen beings. It is instruction for remaining faithful during the collapse of the old covenant power structures.

The Armour Describes What Is Already True (6:14–17)

Stand therefore —

having fastened truth around your waist,

having put on righteousness as a breastplate,

and having fitted your feet with readiness from the gospel of peace.

In every situation, take up the shield of faith, with which you extinguish all the flaming accusations of the evil one.

Take the helmet of salvation and the sword of the Spirit — which is the word of God.

Fulfilled clarification: This armour is not something believers must assemble. It is a metaphor describing the New Covenant resources already supplied in Christ.

Prayer as Ongoing Participation (6:18–20)

With every kind of prayer and request, pray at all times in the Spirit, remaining alert with perseverance and intercession for all the saints.

Pray also for me, that words may be given to me to boldly make known the mystery of the gospel,

for which I am an ambassador in chains, so that I may proclaim it boldly, as I ought to speak.

Closing Peace and Assurance (6:21–24)

So that you also may know how I am doing, Tychicus — a beloved brother and faithful servant in the Lord — will tell you everything.

I have sent him for this purpose: to inform you and to encourage your hearts.

Peace to the brothers and sisters, and love with faith, from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

Grace be with all who love our Lord Jesus Christ with an undying love.

Fulfillment Commentary

1) The battle is about allegiance, not combat

Paul never tells believers to attack powers. He tells them to stand. Victory belongs to Christ; steadfastness belongs to the people.

2) “Rulers and authorities” are covenant powers

These are not rogue demons. They are authority structures — law-based, accusatory, and oppressive — that ruled the old age now judged and displaced.

3) The armour mirrors Isaiah’s fulfilled imagery

Paul draws from Isaiah’s divine warrior language, now fulfilled in Christ and shared with His people.

4) Prayer sustains awareness, not warfare

Prayer keeps believers grounded in truth, peace, and confidence — not locked in anxiety or fear.

New Creation Reality

Believers are not fighting for victory. They are standing in it.

The armour of God is not a daily ritual. It is the description of a people who know who they are and refuse to be shaken by a world that has already been judged.

Reflection

Where am I still reacting as if I must fight for what Christ has already secured — and what would it look like to simply stand?
