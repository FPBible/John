Ephesians 5

Children of Light in a World Already Renewed

Original text: Ephesians 5 — WEB

Immediate Context

Paul continues describing what life looks like when the old covenant world has been put off and the new humanity has been put on.

These instructions are not threats of exclusion or warnings of future condemnation. They describe alignment with a reality that is already true in Christ.

Fulfilled Paraphrase

Imitate God as Beloved Children (5:1–7)

Therefore, be imitators of God as beloved children,

and walk in love, just as Christ loved us and gave Himself for us — a fragrant offering and sacrifice to God.

Sexual immorality, impurity, and greed must not even be named among you, as is fitting for holy people.

Nor should there be obscenity, foolish talk, or coarse joking — things that do not belong — but rather thanksgiving.

For you know this clearly: no one shaped by sexual exploitation, impurity, or greed — which is idolatry — has an inheritance in the kingdom of Christ and God.

Let no one deceive you with empty words, for because of these things the judgment of God came upon the sons of disobedience.

Therefore, do not become partners with them.

Fulfilled clarification: “Inheritance” language here does not threaten believers with future loss. It contrasts two covenant worlds: one marked by idolatry and exploitation, the other by participation in Christ’s reign.

Live as Light, Not Darkness (5:8–14)

For you were once darkness, but now you are light in the Lord. Walk as children of light —

for the fruit of the light consists in all goodness, righteousness, and truth —

testing what is pleasing to the Lord.

Have no partnership with the unfruitful works of darkness, but rather expose them.

For it is shameful even to mention what is done by them in secret.

But everything exposed by the light becomes visible, for light makes everything visible.

Therefore it says: “Awake, sleeper, rise from the dead, and Christ will shine on you.”

Fulfilled clarification: This is resurrection language, applied to present awakening from covenant darkness into New Creation life — not a future bodily event.

Redeem the Time — The Age Has Shifted (5:15–21)

So pay careful attention to how you walk — not as unwise, but as wise —

making the most of the time, because the days were evil.

Therefore do not be foolish, but understand what the will of the Lord is.

Do not get drunk with wine, which leads to excess, but be filled with the Spirit —

speaking to one another with psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs, singing and making melody in your heart to the Lord,

always giving thanks to God the Father for everything, in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ,

submitting to one another out of reverence for Christ.

Fulfilled clarification: “The days were evil” refers to the final phase of the old covenant age. “Redeeming the time” means living wisely during the covenant transition, not anxiously racing toward a future apocalypse.

Marriage as New Covenant Sign (5:22–33)

Wives, align yourselves with your own husbands as to the Lord,

because the husband is head of the wife as Christ is head of the assembly — He Himself being the Saviour of the body.

As the assembly aligns with Christ, so also wives align with their husbands in everything.

Husbands, love your wives, just as Christ loved the assembly and gave Himself up for her,

so that He might cleanse her, having washed her by the word,

so that He might present the assembly to Himself glorious, without spot or wrinkle or anything like that — holy and blameless.

In the same way, husbands are to love their wives as their own bodies.

For no one ever hated their own flesh, but nourishes and cherishes it, just as Christ does the assembly,

because we are members of His body.

“For this reason, a man shall leave his father and mother and be joined to his wife, and the two shall become one flesh.”

This mystery is profound — but I am speaking about Christ and the assembly.

Each one of you must love his wife as himself, and the wife must respect her husband.

Fulfilled clarification: The “presentation” of the assembly is not future perfectionism. It describes the completed covenant cleansing accomplished in Christ, publicly revealed as the old order faded away.

Fulfillment Commentary

1) Ethics flow from identity, not threat

Paul never says, “Do this so you can belong.” He says, “Live like who you already are.”

2) Judgment language is historical and covenantal

“The judgment of God” here refers to the collapse of the old covenant world that persisted in idolatry and injustice. It is not aimed at those in Christ.

3) Resurrection language is applied now

“Awake, sleeper” is present awakening. Resurrection defines New Creation life, not merely its conclusion.

4) Marriage points beyond itself

Marriage is used as a signpost, not a hierarchy blueprint. Its deepest meaning is found in Christ’s self-giving love toward His people.

New Creation Reality

Light does not struggle with darkness. It exposes it.

New Creation ethics are not survival rules. They are expressions of life already shared in Christ.

Ephesians 5 calls believers to live awake, aware, and aligned with a world already made new.
