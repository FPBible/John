Ephesians

New Creation Paraphrase

Reading Frame
Ephesians was written during the final years of the Old Covenant world, addressing believers living through the transition into the fully established New Creation. Paul is not pointing toward a distant future, but interpreting what Christ had already accomplished and what was then being publicly realised.

Ephesians sets out the constitution of the New Creation — identity, union, reconciliation, and life lived from a finished work in Christ. Its language of “inheritance,” “redemption,” and “fullness” belongs to a historical covenant transition, not to an open-ended future expectation.
