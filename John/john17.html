Gospel of John — Fulfilled Paraphrase

Chapter 17 — Glory Shared and the World Made One

Jesus Prays for Himself

When Jesus had spoken these words, He lifted His eyes to heaven and said, “Father, the hour has come. Glorify Your Son, so that the Son may glorify You. You have given Him authority over all flesh, to give eternal life to all whom You have given Him. And this is eternal life: that they know You, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom You have sent” (Jn 17:1–3).

“I glorified You on earth, having finished the work that You gave Me to do. And now, Father, glorify Me in Your presence with the glory I had with You before the world existed” (Jn 17:4–5).

Jesus Prays for the Disciples

“I have revealed Your name to those You gave Me out of the world. They were Yours, and You gave them to Me, and they have kept Your word. Now they know that everything You have given Me comes from You. I have given them the words You gave Me, and they received them and truly knew that I came from You, and they believed that You sent Me” (Jn 17:6–8).

“I am praying for them. I am not praying for the world, but for those You have given Me, for they are Yours. All that is Mine is Yours, and Yours is Mine, and I am glorified in them. I am no longer in the world, but they are in the world, and I am coming to You. Holy Father, keep them in Your name, so that they may be one, just as We are one” (Jn 17:9–11).

“While I was with them, I kept them in Your name. I guarded them, and none of them was lost except the son of destruction, so that Scripture would be fulfilled. Now I am coming to You, and I speak these things in the world so that they may have My joy made complete in themselves” (Jn 17:12–13).

“I have given them Your word, and the world has hated them because they are not of the world, just as I am not of the world. I do not ask that You take them out of the world, but that You keep them from the evil one. They are not of the world, just as I am not of the world. Sanctify them in the truth; Your word is truth. As You sent Me into the world, so I have sent them into the world” (Jn 17:14–18).

“For their sake I set Myself apart, so that they also may be set apart in truth” (Jn 17:19).

Jesus Prays for All Who Will Believe

“I do not ask for these only, but also for those who will believe in Me through their word, so that they may all be one. As You, Father, are in Me, and I am in You, may they also be in Us, so that the world may believe that You sent Me. The glory You gave Me I have given to them, so that they may be one, just as We are one” (Jn 17:20–22).

“I in them, and You in Me, so that they may be brought to full unity. Then the world will know that You sent Me and loved them even as You loved Me. Father, I desire that those You have given Me may be with Me where I am, to see My glory, the glory You have given Me because You loved Me before the foundation of the world” (Jn 17:23–24).

“Righteous Father, though the world does not know You, I know You, and these know that You sent Me. I have made Your name known to them, and will continue to make it known, so that the love with which You loved Me may be in them, and I in them” (Jn 17:25–26).

Fulfilled Commentary — John 17

1. “The hour has come” signals consummation

In John, “the hour” is never vague. It is the decisive moment when the old world collapses and the new begins. This prayer stands at the threshold of cross, resurrection, and vindication — not anticipation, but arrival.

2. Eternal life is present relational participation

Eternal life is defined, not postponed. It is knowing the Father through the Son. This is covenant union now, not survival later.

3. “I have finished the work” is covenant certainty

Jesus speaks of completion before the cross, because covenantally the outcome is settled. History is about to catch up with what is already true.

4. The disciples are transferred, not extracted

They remain in the world geographically, but are no longer of it covenantally. The “world” here is the old covenant order, already judged and passing away.

5. Sanctification is covenant relocation

To be sanctified in truth is to be set apart from the old defining system and established in the revelation of the Son. This is identity, not behaviour management.

6. Oneness is shared divine life, not institutional unity

The unity Jesus prays for mirrors Father–Son union. It is participatory, ontological, and covenantal — not denominational alignment.

7. Glory is already shared

Jesus says He has already given glory. New Creation people do not wait for access or honour. They live from shared life in the Son.

8. “With Me where I am” means present union

This is not future relocation. To be where He is is to share His standing with the Father. Union, not geography, is the point.

9. John 17 is the New Creation charter

This prayer defines identity, secures belonging, establishes unity, and anticipates the collapse of the old world. By AD 70, history confirmed what Jesus prayed.

John 17 is not aspirational. It is constitutional. We do not pray toward it — we live from it.
