Resurrection

A fulfilled (AD 70) evaluation: what “resurrection” means in Scripture, how it was promised, and what it means now in the New Creation.

Bible Index Prophecy Hub New Creation Life Glossary Audio

Thesis: In the Bible, resurrection is God’s covenantal victory over “death” (condemnation/exile under the Law), climaxing at the end of the age in the first-century transition, producing the New Creation life now.

Key texts: 1 Cor 15; Rom 5–8; 2 Cor 3–5; Eph 2; Col 2–3; Heb 8–10, 12; Dan 12; Ezek 37; Isa 25–26; Rev 20–22; Matt 24; Luke 21.

1) Define the Bible’s “Death” and “Life”

Scripture regularly uses “death” and “life” as covenant realities, not merely biological states.

Death as condemnation/exile

  • “The ministry of death, engraved on stones” (2 Cor 3:7)
  • “The sting of death is sin, and the power of sin is the law” (1 Cor 15:56)
  • “You were dead in…sins” (Eph 2:1–5)
  • “The mind of the flesh is death” (Rom 8:6)
  • “The wages of sin is death” (Rom 6:23)

Life as restored access/union

  • “Made us alive together with Christ…raised us up” (Eph 2:5–6)
  • “If anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation” (2 Cor 5:17)
  • “You have died, and your life is hidden with Christ” (Col 3:3)
  • “Passed out of death into life” (John 5:24)
  • “He who has the Son has the life” (1 John 5:11–12)

2) The Old Testament Backdrop: Corporate “Raising” Imagery

The prophets often describe covenant restoration using resurrection language.

  • Ezekiel 37 — Israel’s restoration pictured as bones coming to life (Ezek 37:1–14)
  • Isaiah 26 — “Your dead shall live” in the context of deliverance and vindication (Isa 26:19)
  • Isaiah 25 — death “swallowed up” with covenant comfort for God’s people (Isa 25:6–9)
  • Hosea 6 — “After two days…on the third day he will raise us up” (Hos 6:1–2)

These passages are not presented as random biology lessons. They are covenant images: death/exile → restoration/life; shame → vindication; separation → access.

3) Daniel 12 and “the End”: Resurrection Tied to the Covenant Crisis

Daniel’s resurrection language is anchored to a specific time of national trouble and deliverance.

  • “There shall be a time of trouble…at that time your people shall be delivered” (Dan 12:1)
  • “Many of those who sleep…shall awake” (Dan 12:2)
  • End markers: “at the end of the days” / the finishing of Daniel’s vision (Dan 12:4, 9, 13)

In the New Testament, Jesus and the apostles place the climactic “end” within the first-century generation:

  • “This generation will not pass away until all these things are accomplished” (Matt 24:34; Luke 21:32)
  • “The end of the ages has come” (1 Cor 10:11)
  • “It is the last hour” (1 John 2:18)
  • “The consummation of the ages” (Heb 9:26)

4) 1 Corinthians 15: What Resurrection Does (and What It Isn’t)

Paul’s chapter is the spine for resurrection doctrine. Read it in Paul’s own categories: Adam/Christ, death/life, corruption/incorruption, earthly/heavenly.

A) Resurrection and the defeat of “death”

  • “As in Adam all die, so also in Christ all will be made alive” (1 Cor 15:22)
  • “The last enemy that will be abolished is death” (1 Cor 15:26)
  • “The sting of death is sin, and the power of sin is the law” (1 Cor 15:56)

Paul locates death’s authority in sin empowered by the Law. Resurrection therefore includes the removal of that condemning power.

B) “Flesh and blood” and the kind of body

  • “Flesh and blood can’t inherit the Kingdom of God” (1 Cor 15:50)
  • “It is sown a natural body; it is raised a spiritual body” (1 Cor 15:44)
  • “The first man is of the earth… the second man is of heaven” (1 Cor 15:47)

Paul contrasts two modes of humanity: Adamic (earthly/condemned) and Christic (heavenly/life). The resurrection body is the new covenant identity of God’s people in Christ.

C) Timing anchored to “the end”

  • “Then comes the end” (1 Cor 15:24)
  • “We will not all sleep, but we will all be changed” (1 Cor 15:51)

Paul speaks as a first-century apostle within the “end of the age” horizon (1 Cor 10:11; Heb 9:26).

5) “Already Raised” Texts: Resurrection as Present Reality

The New Testament repeatedly speaks of resurrection life as something believers possess now.

  • “Made us alive… and raised us up with him” (Eph 2:5–6)
  • “Buried with him… raised with him through faith” (Col 2:12–13)
  • “If then you were raised together with Christ” (Col 3:1–4)
  • “Has passed out of death into life” (John 5:24)
  • “We know that we have passed out of death into life” (1 John 3:14)

If resurrection is only future, these passages become awkward or “just positional talk.” Scripture treats them as real: a present participation in Christ’s victory.

6) Hebrews: The Covenant Shift That Ends the Old “Death-Order”

Hebrews frames the transition from the old system to the new as an actual shift of access, standing, and covenant reality.

  • Old covenant “becoming old and growing aged” and “near to vanishing away” (Heb 8:13)
  • Christ appears “at the consummation of the ages” to put away sin (Heb 9:26)
  • One offering perfects forever and removes the need for repeat sacrifices (Heb 10:10–14)
  • “The way into the holy place was not yet made manifest” while the first tabernacle stood (Heb 9:8)
  • Believers come to the heavenly Jerusalem now (Heb 12:22–24)

7) Revelation: Death Removed, Life Unveiled

Revelation’s arc moves from covenant judgment to New Creation life: the removal of death’s rule and the unveiling of God-with-us.

  • “Blessed and holy is he who has part in the first resurrection” (Rev 20:6)
  • “Death and Hades were thrown into the lake of fire” (Rev 20:14)
  • New creation climax: “death will be no more” (Rev 21:4)
  • “Behold, I make all things new” (Rev 21:5)
  • “The tabernacle of God is with men” (Rev 21:3)

8) What Resurrection Means Now

  • Freedom: condemnation is not your operating environment (Rom 8:1–4)
  • Identity: you are new creation, not “old man with a religious add-on” (2 Cor 5:17; Eph 4:22–24)
  • Holiness: you live from life, not fear of death (Rom 6:4–11; Col 3:1–10)
  • Assurance: resurrection life is Christ’s present gift (John 11:25–26; 1 John 5:11–13)
  • Mission: reconciliation is the fruit of resurrection life (2 Cor 5:18–21)

Plainly: we don’t “wait to become alive.” We live because life has been given. Resurrection is not a distant carrot; it is the covenant reality of the New Creation in Christ.
