Resurrection

From Covenant Death to Life in Christ · Fulfilled Horizon

Resurrection in Scripture is not treated as a vague religious hope. It is the covenant victory of God in Christ: death (condemnation/exile under the Law) is ended, and life (access/union) is established as present reality.

Rule: if “resurrection” is pushed into an undefined future, it quietly rebuilds the distance Christ ended. FPBible ties resurrection to the Bible’s stated horizon: the end of the age and the covenant transition.

Thesis in One Paragraph

What we mean by “resurrection” in this project.

In Scripture, death is not only physical dying; it is also covenant separation and condemnation under sin empowered by the Law (1 Cor 15:56, 2 Cor 3:7, Eph 2:1). Resurrection is God raising His people from that death-order into Christ’s life-order—access, union, and Spirit-life as present reality (Eph 2:5–6, Col 2:12–13, Col 3:1). Paul ties the defeat of “death” to the end of the age horizon and the removal of the Law’s condemning power (1 Cor 15:24–26, Heb 9:26, Heb 8:13).

Key Texts (What They Actually Say)

Concise, but serious — follow the argument inside each text.
1 Corinthians 15

Death defeated where its power lives

Paul defines death’s sting and power: sin and Law. Resurrection is not framed as “flesh upgrading,” but as victory over condemnation and corruption.

  • 1 Cor 15:20–28 (order → “then comes the end”)
  • 1 Cor 15:42–50 (natural/spiritual; flesh and blood)
  • 1 Cor 15:51–57 (“power of sin is the law”)
Ephesians & Colossians

Raised with Christ — present tense reality

These letters don’t treat resurrection as only future. They ground ethics in a present “raised” identity.

  • Eph 2:1–6 (dead → alive → raised)
  • Col 2:12–15 (raised; record of debt cancelled)
  • Col 3:1–4 (if you were raised… set your mind)
Hebrews

Covenant transition: access replaces shadow

Hebrews describes the old covenant as aging and vanishing, and the “consummation of the ages” as present to them.

  • Heb 8:13 (near to vanishing)
  • Heb 9:8–10 (access not yet opened under the first)
  • Heb 9:26–28 (consummation of the ages)
  • Heb 10:10–14 (perfected by one offering)
  • Heb 12:22–24 (you have come to the heavenly Jerusalem)
Prophets

Resurrection language as corporate restoration

The prophets regularly use “raise / live / awake” as covenant restoration imagery.

  • Ezek 37:1–14 (bones → Israel restored)
  • Isa 25:6–9 (death swallowed up)
  • Isa 26:19 (your dead shall live)
  • Dan 12:1–3 (trouble → deliverance → awakening)
Jesus’ Horizon

“This generation” and the end of the age

Jesus anchors the crisis language (judgment, coming, end) inside that generation’s horizon.

  • Matt 24:34 (this generation)
  • Luke 21:32 (this generation)
  • Matt 16:27–28 (some standing here…)
  • Matt 23:36 (all these things…this generation)
Revelation

Death removed as New Creation reality

Revelation moves from covenant judgment to New Jerusalem life, with “death” removed as an order.

  • Rev 20:6 (first resurrection)
  • Rev 20:14 (death and Hades removed)
  • Rev 21:3–5 (death no more; all things new)
  • Rev 22:1–2 (river and tree of life)

Logic (No Hand-waving)

If these links break, the doctrine drifts.
  • Death’s power is Law-based condemnation: 1 Cor 15:56, 2 Cor 3:7–11, Rom 7:9–11.
  • Christ ends condemnation and introduces Spirit-life now: Rom 8:1–4, Eph 2:5–6, Col 3:1–4.
  • Resurrection belongs to the “end of the age” horizon: 1 Cor 15:24, Heb 9:26, 1 Cor 10:11, Matt 24:34.
  • Therefore: resurrection is not a perpetual “almost.” It is the covenant victory that makes New Creation life normal.

This does not deny that people physically die. It denies that “resurrection” must be reduced to biology, when Scripture defines the problem (death) and its power (law) in covenant terms.

What This Means Now

Resurrection is not trivia; it’s the foundation for living.
Freedom

No condemnation

Resurrection life is lived without law-fear as the operating system.

  • Rom 8:1
  • Rom 6:11
Identity

New humanity

You live from a raised identity, not to earn one.

  • 2 Cor 5:17
  • Col 3:1–3
Holiness

Life expressed

Holiness is life expressed, not fear-managed performance.

  • Rom 6:4
  • Col 3:5–10
Mission

Reconciliation

Resurrection life becomes reconciliation in the world.

  • 2 Cor 5:18–20
  • Rom 5:10–11

Want the short version? Resurrection means: you are not waiting to become alive. You are called to live awake, because life has already been given in Christ.
