Resurrection

From Covenant Death to Life in Christ · Fulfilled Horizon

Resurrection in Scripture is not treated as vague religious optimism. It is God’s covenant victory in Christ: death (condemnation/exile under the Law) is ended, and life (access/union by the Spirit) is established as present reality.

FPBible rule: if “resurrection” is pushed into an undefined future, it quietly rebuilds the distance Christ ended. The apostles tie resurrection to the Bible’s stated horizon: the end of the age and the covenant transition.

Thesis in One Paragraph

What we mean by “resurrection” in this project.

In Scripture, death is not only physical dying; it is covenant separation and condemnation under sin empowered by the Law (1 Cor 15:56, 2 Cor 3:7–11, Eph 2:1). Resurrection is God raising His people from that death-order into Christ’s life-order—access, union, and Spirit-life as present reality (Eph 2:5–6, Col 2:12–13, Col 3:1). Paul ties the defeat of “death” to the end-of-the-age horizon and the removal of the Law’s condemning power (1 Cor 15:24–26, Heb 9:26, Heb 8:13, 1 Cor 10:11).

The Body in 1 Corinthians 15

Paul’s “body” language is covenant-corporate — the old body dies; the new body rises.
Paul’s categories

He contrasts two “humanities”

Paul sets resurrection inside the Adam → Christ contrast. That is not a lab description of tissue; it is a covenant identity contrast: the old humanity under sin/death versus the new humanity in Christ.

  • 1 Cor 15:21–22 (in Adam / in Christ)
  • 1 Cor 15:45–49 (first man / last man; image)
  • Rom 5:12–21 (two reigns)
  • Rom 6:4–11 (old man crucified; newness of life)

Resurrection “body” sits inside this corporate storyline: an old order/personhood dies; a new one lives.

One Body

The “resurrection body” is the Body of Christ

Paul does not invent “body” language in chapter 15. He has already defined the Church as Christ’s body: one corporate people, animated by one Spirit, formed by union with the risen Lord. The “new body” is not individual flesh upgraded; it is the new covenant people constituted in Christ.

  • 1 Cor 12:12–27 (you are the body of Christ)
  • Rom 12:4–5 (one body in Christ)
  • Eph 1:22–23 (Church as His body)
  • Eph 2:14–16 (one new man; hostility abolished)
  • Col 1:18 (He is head of the body)

If Paul’s “body” in 12 is covenant-corporate (it is), then his “body” in 15 must be read within the same framework.

Sowing / raising

“Sown… raised” describes covenant transformation

Paul’s seed metaphor is about continuity-with-transformation: what is “sown” is not identical in form to what is “raised,” but it is organically related. In covenant terms, the old covenant body (Israel under Law and condemnation) is “sown” in corruption; the new covenant body (the Church in Christ) is “raised” in incorruption.

  • 1 Cor 15:35–38 (seed analogy)
  • 1 Cor 15:42–44 (sown/raised; natural/spiritual)
  • 2 Cor 5:17 (new creation)
  • Heb 8:13 (old covenant vanishing)

Paul’s question is not “what will my atoms do?” but “how does God bring His people through death into life?”

“Flesh and blood”

Not a denial of bodies — a denial of inheritance by old-order identity

“Flesh and blood cannot inherit the Kingdom” is covenant language: the old identity (Adam / Law / corruption) cannot inherit the new order. Resurrection is the transition into a Spirit-governed inheritance.

  • 1 Cor 15:50 (flesh and blood)
  • Gal 4:29–31 (bondwoman/free; inheritance)
  • Rom 8:14–17 (sons; heirs)
  • Heb 12:22–24 (you have come…)
Covenant Body summary

Paul’s “body” logic is consistent across his letters: one corporate people. The old covenant body lived under condemnation and death’s reign; the new covenant body is Christ’s body, alive by the Spirit. Resurrection, therefore, is not a detached future event — it is the covenant raising of God’s people into the Church of Christ.

  • 1 Cor 15:56 (death’s power defined)
  • Eph 2:15–16 (one new man)
  • Col 2:14–15 (debt cancelled; powers disarmed)
  • 2 Cor 3:6–11 (death ministry fading; Spirit ministry remaining)

This doesn’t deny that humans die physically. It denies that “resurrection” must be reduced to biology when Paul locates death’s sting in sin empowered by the Law and describes resurrection life as present union in Christ.

Key Texts (What They Actually Say)

Concise, but serious — follow the argument inside each text.
1 Corinthians 15

Death defeated where its power lives

Paul defines death’s sting and power: sin and Law. Resurrection is framed as victory over condemnation/corruption — then “the end” within their horizon.

  • 1 Cor 15:20–28
  • 1 Cor 15:42–50
  • 1 Cor 15:51–57
Ephesians & Colossians

Raised with Christ — present tense reality

Ethics is grounded in a present “raised” identity: “if you were raised…”

  • Eph 2:1–6
  • Col 2:12–15
  • Col 3:1–4
Hebrews

Covenant transition: access replaces shadow

The old covenant ages and vanishes; the “consummation of the ages” is in their time.

  • Heb 8:13
  • Heb 9:8–10
  • Heb 9:26–28
  • Heb 12:22–24
Prophets

Resurrection language as corporate restoration

Prophets use “raise / live / awake” as covenant restoration imagery.

  • Ezek 37:1–14
  • Isa 25:6–9
  • Isa 26:19
  • Dan 12:1–3
Jesus’ Horizon

“This generation” and the end of the age

The crisis language is anchored inside that generation’s horizon.

  • Matt 24:34
  • Luke 21:32
  • Matt 16:27–28
  • Matt 23:36
Revelation

Death removed as New Creation reality

Revelation moves from covenant judgment to New Jerusalem life, with “death” removed as an order.

  • Rev 20:6
  • Rev 20:14
  • Rev 21:3–5
  • Rev 22:1–2

Logic (No Hand-waving)

If these links break, the doctrine drifts.
  • Death’s power is Law-based condemnation: 1 Cor 15:56, 2 Cor 3:7–11, Rom 7:9–11.
  • The old covenant order “dies” and is replaced by Spirit-life: Heb 8:13, Heb 9:26, 2 Cor 3:6–11.
  • Christ ends condemnation and introduces Spirit-life now: Rom 8:1–4, Eph 2:5–6, Col 3:1–4.
  • The “body” Paul preaches is one corporate people in Christ: 1 Cor 12:27, Eph 2:15–16, Col 1:18.
  • Therefore: resurrection is not a perpetual “almost.” It is the covenant victory that makes New Creation life normal.

This does not deny that people physically die. It denies that “resurrection” must be reduced to biology, when Paul defines death’s sting in sin empowered by the Law and describes resurrection life as present union in Christ.

What This Means Now

Resurrection is not trivia; it’s the foundation for living.
Freedom

No condemnation

Resurrection life is lived without law-fear as the operating system.

  • Rom 8:1
  • Rom 6:11
Identity

New humanity

You live from a raised identity, not to earn one.

  • 2 Cor 5:17
  • Col 3:1–3
Holiness

Life expressed

Holiness is life expressed, not fear-managed performance.

  • Rom 6:4
  • Col 3:5–10
Mission

Reconciliation

Resurrection life becomes reconciliation in the world.

  • 2 Cor 5:18–20
  • Rom 5:10–11

Resurrection means: you are not waiting to become alive. You are called to live awake, because life has already been given in Christ.
