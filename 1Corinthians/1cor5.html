1 Corinthians 5

Holiness as Identity · The End of Tolerated Corruption

Fulfilled Paraphrase

Paul addresses a case of sexual immorality so severe that even the surrounding culture finds it offensive. The community is not grieving — it is boasting. Paul confronts this as a failure of identity, not a lack of rules.

Though absent in body, Paul declares a decisive judgment: the behaviour cannot continue unchecked within the community. The goal is not destruction, but the exposure of what does not belong so that restoration remains possible.

Paul invokes Passover imagery. A little leaven works through the whole batch. Christ, the Passover lamb, has already been sacrificed. Therefore the community must live as it truly is — unleavened.

He clarifies that separation is not from the world, but from persistent, unrepentant behaviour within the body. God judges those outside. The community is responsible for protecting its shared life.

Immediate Context

Corinth is confusing freedom with indifference. Paul corrects this by showing that New Covenant freedom does not ignore destructive behaviour. It addresses it without reverting to law.

Fulfilled Commentary

1) Judgment here is covenantal, not punitive

Paul is not handing someone over to eternal torment. He is removing covenant protection so the seriousness of the behaviour becomes visible. This is corrective exposure, not vengeance.

2) “Deliver to Satan” is exclusion language

“Satan” functions here as the realm outside the covenant community — the sphere where destructive patterns dominate. The aim is repentance, not condemnation.

3) Leaven imagery assumes completion

Paul does not say, “Become unleavened.” He says, “You are unleavened.” Holiness flows from identity already established in Christ.

4) Separation protects life, not reputation

This is not image management. It is community care. Unchecked corruption spreads and damages everyone. Love confronts for the sake of restoration.

Key Cross-References

  • Exodus 12 — Passover and leaven
  • Galatians 5 — leaven and freedom
  • Hebrews 12 — discipline as sonship

New Creation Reality

Holiness is not enforced by fear. It is protected by truth. In Christ, the community does not pretend corruption is harmless. It confronts it without losing sight of restoration.

Reflection

Where might I be mistaking tolerance for love, or silence for freedom?
