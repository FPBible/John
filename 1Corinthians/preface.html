1 Corinthians — Preface

How to Read This Letter (New Creation Lens)

1 Corinthians is not a law manual. It is a New Covenant correction letter. Paul addresses real dysfunction, not by reinstating Torah, but by re-centering everything on Christ and the Spirit.

1) This Letter Is About Identity Before Behaviour

Paul repeatedly corrects people by reminding them who they are: sanctified in Christ, temples of the Spirit, members of one body, participants in one table, heirs of one victory. When identity is lost, behaviour collapses.

2) “Wisdom” vs “Cross” Is a World-Collision

Corinth loved status, rhetoric, and public dominance. Paul dismantles that system with the cross. The cross is not a religious symbol here. It is the end of human boasting, the collapse of old status games, and the foundation of a new kind of community.

3) The Spirit Is the Operating System

Paul does not tell them to build holiness by external pressure. He tells them to walk in Spirit-life: love, maturity, self-control, and edification. Gifts are not for spectacle. They are for building up the body.

4) Resurrection Language Must Be Read Covenantally

1 Corinthians 15 is the major “resurrection” chapter. In this project, resurrection language is read the way Scripture uses it: as victory, transformation, and covenant transition, not necessarily as biological mechanics in a distant future. Paul’s argument is that Christ’s victory has redefined life, identity, and belonging in the new age.

How to Read 1 Corinthians Here

  • Keep the setting: a mixed community inside a collapsing old world
  • Read correction through union, not through shame
  • Let love be the interpretive centre (ch. 13)
  • Read “resurrection” with the gospel’s finished work as the anchor

This letter is messy because people were messy. The point is not perfection. The point is New Covenant life replacing old identity systems.
