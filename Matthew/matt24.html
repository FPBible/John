Matthew 24

The Deconstruction of the Old and the Arrival of the New

Immediate Context

Jesus has just left the Temple for the final time, pronouncing it "desolate" (23:38). As He sits on the Mount of Olives overlooking Jerusalem, the disciples ask for the "timing" and "signs" of the Temple's destruction and the consummation of the Mosaic Age. This chapter, the Olivet Discourse, provides the blueprint for the transition from the Old Covenant to the New.

1And Jesus having gone out, went from the temple, and His disciples came near to show Him the buildings of the temple, 2and Jesus said to them, "Do you not see all these? Truly I say to you, there may not be left here a stone on a stone, that will not be thrown down." 3And as He is sitting on the Mount of Olives, the disciples came near to Him by themselves, saying, "Tell us, when will these be? And what is the sign of Your presence, and of the completion of the age?" 4And Jesus answering said to them, "See that no one may lead you astray, 5for many will come in My Name, saying, I am the Christ, and they will lead many astray; 6and you are about to hear of wars and rumors of wars; see, be not troubled, for it is necessary for all these to come to pass, but the end is not yet. 7For nation will rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom, and there will be famines, and pestilences, and earthquakes, in various places; 8and all these are the beginning of birth-pains; 9then they will deliver you up to tribulation, and will kill you, and you will be hated by all the nations because of My Name; 10and then will many be stumbled, and they will deliver up one another, and they will hate one another. 11And many false prophets will arise, and will lead many astray; 12and because of the abounding of the lawlessness, the love of the many will become cold; 13but he who has endured to the end—he will be saved; 14and this Good News of the Kingdom will be proclaimed in all the world, for a testimony to all the nations, and then will the end come. 15Whenever, therefore, you may see the abomination of the desolation, that was spoken of through Daniel the prophet, standing in the holy place (whoever is reading let him observe), 16then those in Judea, let them flee to the mountains; 17he on the house-top, let him not come down to take anything out of his house; 18and he in the field, let him not turn back to take his garments. 19And woe to those with child, and to those giving suck in those days; 20and pray that your flight may not be in winter, nor on a Sabbath; 21for then there will be great tribulation, such as has not been from the beginning of the world until now, no, nor may ever be. 22And if those days were not shortened, no flesh would have been saved; but because of the chosen, those days will be shortened. 23Then if any may say to you, Behold, here is the Christ! or, Here! you may not believe it; 24for there will arise false Christs and false prophets, and they will give great signs and wonders, so as to lead astray, if possible, also the chosen. 25Behold, I have foretold it to you. 26If, therefore, they may say to you, Behold, he is in the wilderness, you may not go forth; Behold, in the inner chambers, you may not believe it; 27for as the lightning comes out from the east, and appears to the west, so will be also the presence of the Son of Man; 28for wherever the carcass may be, there will the eagles be gathered together. 29And immediately after the tribulation of those days, the sun will be darkened, and the moon will not give her light, and the stars will fall from the heaven, and the powers of the heavens will be shaken; 30and then will appear the sign of the Son of Man in the heaven; and then will all the tribes of the earth mourn, and they will see the Son of Man coming on the clouds of the heaven with power and much glory; 31and He will send His messengers with a great sound of a trumpet, and they will gather together His chosen from the four winds, from the ends of the heavens to their ends. 32And from the fig tree learn the parable: when its branch may have already become tender, and may put forth the leaves, you know that the summer is near, 33so also you, when you may see all these, know that it is near—at the doors. 34Truly I say to you, this generation may by no means pass away until all these may come to pass. 35The heaven and the earth will pass away, but My words will by no means pass away.
Completion of the Age (Sunteleia): The termination of the Mosaic economy and its ritual system, not the end of the physical planet.
Presence (Parousia): Christ's arrival in judgment upon Jerusalem (AD 70), confirming His enthronement in heaven.
All the World (Oikoumene): The inhabited Roman world, as fulfilled in Colossians 1:6 and 1:23.
Abomination of Desolation: The Roman armies (Luke 21:20) surrounding and polluting the holy city.

Fulfillment Commentary

1. The Two-Part Question

The disciples ask about (1) when "these things" (the Temple's destruction) would happen and (2) what the sign of His presence and the "end of the age" would be. In the Jewish mind, the destruction of the Temple *was* the end of the age. Jesus answers these questions as a singular historical event. He is not describing the end of the world, but the end of the Old Covenant "world" centered in Jerusalem.

2. The Sign of the Son of Man

In verse 30, the "sign of the Son of Man in heaven" is not a physical object in the sky. It is the destruction of Jerusalem itself that serves as the sign. The falling of the Temple was the historical proof that the Son of Man had indeed been enthroned in the heavens. When the earthly throne (the Temple) was destroyed, it revealed the heavenly throne (the Christ) as the true seat of authority.

3. De-coding Apocalyptic Language

The darkening of the sun, moon, and stars (v. 29) is standard prophetic imagery for the collapse of political and religious rulers (see Isaiah 13:10 for the fall of Babylon). "Heaven and earth" passing away (v. 35) refers to the Covenantal universe—the Temple and the Law system. When the High Priest's "heavenly" authority and the "earthly" sacrifices ended, that "world" passed away to make room for the unshakeable Kingdom.

4. The Immutable Deadline

Jesus provides the definitive time-stamp in verse 34: "This generation will by no means pass away until all these things take place." To push these events into a distant future is to make Jesus a false prophet. "This generation" always refers to the contemporaries of the speaker. Every sign, including the Great Tribulation and the Parousia, was fulfilled within the 40-year window (AD 30-70).

New Creation Reality

We do not live in fear of a future "Great Tribulation." That historical storm has already passed, clearing the way for the New Heaven and New Earth where righteousness dwells. We live in the post-AD 70 reality where the King is fully enthroned and the way to the Father is open without Temple, priest, or sacrifice. The Kingdom is not "coming"; it is here, and it is expanding.

Reflection

If Jesus fulfilled every promise of judgment on the Old system exactly when He said He would, how much more can you trust His present-tense promise to be with you until the very completion of your own purpose in His Kingdom?
