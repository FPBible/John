Prophecy Hub

Fulfilled Judgment · Covenant Transition · New Creation

Biblical prophecy does not describe the end of the physical universe. It announces covenant judgment, the removal of the Old Covenant order, and the establishment of the New Covenant world in Christ.

This hub exists to explain how Scripture itself uses prophetic language and how the New Testament declares its fulfilment.

How This Hub Is Structured

The Prophecy Hub is organised into two complementary layers:

  • Framework Pages — establish how prophetic language works across Scripture
  • Textual Deep Dives — examine specific passages phrase by phrase

Framework pages provide orientation. Deep dives provide control. Both are necessary.

I. Covenant Judgment & Transition (Framework Pages)

These pages explain how prophetic language announces judgment on the Old Covenant world and the transition into New Creation life. They are thematic and foundational.

  • Matthew 24 — Judgment on Jerusalem
  • Luke 21 — Days of Vengeance
  • Daniel — Kingdom, Son of Man, and Time
  • Isaiah — New Heavens, New Earth, New Humanity
  • Ezekiel 37 — Resurrection as Restoration
  • Psalms — Enthronement and Rule
  • 2 Peter 3 — Heavens and Earth
  • Revelation — Symbol, Judgment, Victory

II. Resurrection Explained (Framework)

These pages establish how resurrection language functions covenantally in Scripture and how it is fulfilled in Christ.

  • Resurrection Explained — 1 Corinthians 15 (Framework)

III. Textual Deep Dives

These pages examine specific prophetic passages line by line and phrase by phrase. They are designed to resolve disputed texts without importing later assumptions.

  • 1 Corinthians 15 — Resurrection Fulfilled (Deep Dive)

IV. Presence, Parousia, and Kingdom

These texts clarify how Scripture describes Christ’s “coming,” kingdom, and reign — not as physical escape, but as revealed presence and covenant authority.

  • 1 Thessalonians 4 — Presence, Not Escape

How to Read Prophetic Language

Prophetic imagery is interpreted by Scripture itself. Where the New Testament explains the meaning of:

  • resurrection
  • judgment
  • kingdom
  • new creation

those explanations govern how prophetic language is read. Symbol is not dismissed — it is decoded by covenant context.
