The End of the Law — Covenant Jurisdiction After Christ

Bible Prophecy Hub Parousia

This page is written to settle one foundational question: Does Sinai still have jurisdiction over believers in Christ? The FP Bible answer is direct: No. Christ fulfilled the Mosaic covenant, removed its legal claims, and established the New Covenant reality that matured through the first-century covenant transition (AD 30–70).

Why this matters: Many modern systems (especially covenant theology and theonomy) end up reinstalling Sinai as the governing framework. That move reshapes how people read Jesus, Paul, Hebrews, prophecy, ethics, and even “holiness.” If Sinai still rules, the cross did not actually change covenant jurisdiction.

Core Thesis

The Mosaic Law was a covenant administration given to Israel, tied to temple, priesthood, sacrifices, land, and national boundary-markers. It was never presented as an eternal constitution for the nations or the church.

In Christ, that covenant reached its intended goal and termination: its condemning “handwriting of requirements” was removed (Colossians 2:14), and believers now live under the New Covenant administration of the Spirit (2 Corinthians 3:6).

What “End of the Law” Means (and What It Doesn’t)

It means

  • Jurisdiction changed: (Romans 6:14, Galatians 5:18)
  • Condemnation ended: (Romans 7:1–6)
  • Identity changed: (Galatians 3, Ephesians 2:11–22)
  • Worship changed: (Hebrews 7–10)

It does not mean

  • “Anything goes” (Romans 8)
  • God changed character (James 1:17)
  • Morality vanished (2 Corinthians 3:3–11)
