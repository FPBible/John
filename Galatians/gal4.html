Galatians 4

Adoption Accomplished · The End of the Guardian

Fulfilled Paraphrase (Galatians 4)

4:1–7 As long as an heir is a child, he differs little from a slave, even though he owns everything. He is under guardians and managers until the time set by his father. In the same way, when we were children, we were enslaved under the elemental principles of the world. But when the fullness of the time came, God sent His Son, born of a woman, born under the law, to redeem those under the law, so that we might receive adoption as sons. Because you are sons, God sent the Spirit of His Son into our hearts, crying, “Abba, Father.” So you are no longer a slave, but a son—and if a son, then an heir through God.

4:8–11 Formerly, when you did not know God, you were enslaved to things that by nature are not gods. But now that you have come to know God—or rather, to be known by God—how can you turn back again to the weak and worthless elemental principles? You observe days, months, seasons, and years. I fear for you, that my labor for you may have been in vain.

4:12–16 Brothers and sisters, I plead with you—become as I am, for I became as you are. You did me no wrong. You know it was because of a physical weakness that I first proclaimed the gospel to you. You did not despise or reject me, but received me as an angel of God, as Christ Jesus Himself. What has happened to your joy? I testify that, if possible, you would have torn out your eyes and given them to me. Have I now become your enemy by telling you the truth?

4:17–20 Those people are zealous for you, but not for good. They want to isolate you, so that you will be zealous for them. It is always good to be zealous for what is good—but not only when I am present with you. My children, for whom I am again in the pains of childbirth until Christ is formed in you—how I wish I could be present with you now and change my tone, because I am perplexed about you.

4:21–31 Tell me, you who want to be under the law—do you not listen to the law? It is written that Abraham had two sons: one by the slave woman and one by the free woman. The son by the slave was born according to the flesh; the son by the free woman through promise. These things are being spoken figuratively. The women represent two covenants. One is from Mount Sinai, bearing children into slavery—this is Hagar. Hagar corresponds to present Jerusalem, for she is in slavery with her children. But the Jerusalem above is free, and she is our mother. For it is written: “Rejoice, barren woman who does not give birth.” Now you, brothers and sisters, like Isaac, are children of promise. But just as then the child born according to the flesh persecuted the child born according to the Spirit, so also now. What does the Scripture say? “Cast out the slave woman and her son.” Therefore, brothers and sisters, we are not children of the slave woman, but of the free woman.

Immediate Context (Who / What / Why)

Paul moves from theology to urgency. Having shown that the law cannot give inheritance, he now exposes the emotional and relational cost of returning to it. The Galatians are not just confused—they are being pulled back into slavery after adoption has already been granted.

Fulfilled Commentary (New Covenant / New Creation Lens)

1) “Fullness of time” marks covenant arrival

This is not a vague spiritual moment. It is the historical completion point of the old covenant’s role. When Christ came, redemption and adoption were accomplished, not postponed.

2) Adoption is finished, not progressive

Paul does not say believers are becoming sons. He says they are sons. The Spirit crying “Abba” is evidence of status already granted. Returning to the law denies that adoption is complete.

3) “Elemental principles” include law-based religion

Paul places paganism and Torah calendar observance in the same category: enslaving systems. Anything that governs identity through external regulation belongs to the old order.

4) Law-teachers isolate to control

Paul exposes the tactic. Law-based influence thrives on separation and dependence. Grace creates freedom and shared belonging; law recreates hierarchy and gatekeeping.

5) Hagar and Sarah represent two covenant worlds

This is not random allegory. Paul explicitly says these women represent covenants. Sinai produces slavery. Promise produces freedom. To return to Sinai is to deny one’s true mother.

6) “Jerusalem above” is New Covenant identity

This is not a future city descending from the sky. It is covenant identity. Believers belong to the free, Spirit-governed order that replaces the old Jerusalem-centered system.

Key Idioms & Language Notes

  • “Elemental principles” — World-order systems that govern identity externally.
  • “Jerusalem above” — Covenant identity, not a literal location.
  • “Adoption” — Legal status change, not emotional maturity.

New Creation Reality (Today)

Galatians 4 calls you to live from belonging, not toward it. If you are in Christ, you are already a son, already an heir, already free. Any system that places you back under supervision is denying the Spirit who already cries “Abba” within you.

Reflection

Where might I still be living as a slave in a house where I am already a son?
