Galatians — Preface

Why This Letter Matters

Galatians is written in crisis. Paul is not clarifying a minor disagreement; he is confronting what he calls a “different gospel.”

The issue is not immorality. It is covenant confusion. Believers who began in Christ are being pressured to return to the law as a means of identity and righteousness.

1) Galatians Is About Covenant, Not Behaviour

Paul’s argument is not about improving conduct through rules. It is about which covenant defines life. To return to the law is not maturity — it is regression.

Galatians insists that Christ does not supplement the law. He replaces it.

2) The Law Has a Defined End

The law is described as a guardian, a tutor, and a temporary custodian. It had a purpose, and that purpose has been fulfilled.

To rebuild what has ended is to deny what Christ has accomplished.

3) New Creation Is the Verdict

Galatians does not leave the issue abstract. Paul states the conclusion plainly:

“Neither circumcision nor uncircumcision means anything. What counts is new creation.”

This is not poetic encouragement. It is a covenant verdict.

4) The Spirit Replaces the Law

Life in Galatians is not managed by Torah. It is animated by the Spirit. The Spirit is not an aid to law-keeping; the Spirit is the new operating system.

This is why Paul contrasts: flesh vs Spirit, slave vs son, law vs promise.

How to Read Galatians Here

  • Assume fulfillment, not delay
  • Read “law” covenantally, not morally
  • Do not soften Paul’s language
  • Let New Creation mean exactly what it says

Galatians is not gentle. But it is freeing.
